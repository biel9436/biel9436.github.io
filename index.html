About

 Hello there! Thanks for stopping by. My name is Daniel Thomas Bielejeski, I am a current senior at the University of St. Thomas studying Computer Science and Philosophy. Computer Science runs in the family, myself, my dad, and two of my brothers all work in the field. I always assumed that I could pick it up quickly and during my first class I fell in love with it! Why Philosophy you ask? Philosophy has always been an interest of mine, I love to ponder the questions it asks and the thinking it requires. It also is a good preparation for my other passion: law.
 Computer Science and Law do not seem related at first, but once you dive deeper they share more than you expect. Both require attention to detail, creative thinking, and problem solving. How I think about computer science problems is the same as I think about about law issues. After college I am considering going to law school at Notre Dame, the University of Minnesota, or Harvard School of Law. I have not completely decided if that is what I want to do, I may take a couple years to work in Computer Science and really gain a clear understanding of the field.
 A few fun facts! I come from a big family, I am the 8th of 11 children. I currently have 20 nieces and nephews. They are a huge blessing in my life. I enjoy playing tennis, reading science fiction, and playing Spikeball. I love to vacation in Florida, or anywhere on the ocean. One day I hope to travel to all 50 states, only 10 left to go!
